Cooperative Collaboration Encasement

Distillation for the field at `cabal-is-coherent.com`.

This document is not a product brief.
It is a held shape for what this space is, what it is for, and what must remain true as it is built.

## What This Is

A field.

A dark space where people leave things: writing, music, art.

No accounts.
No profiles.
No requirement to prove belonging.
No guarantee of permanence.

Things are held here by attention.
When attention moves away, they darken.
When their time runs out, they are gone.

What stays, stays because someone is still here with it.

That is the core proposition.

## Where This Sits

This domain is not the threshold.
The threshold happens elsewhere.

People arrive here either by traveling through `coherent-cabal.com` or by being given a direct link from someone already in relation to the larger space.

By the time they reach this field, they do not need more initiation.
They do not need more puzzles.
They do not need more unnecessary friction.

This domain is an arrival space.
An integration space.
A real place for humans to connect online.

## What The Space Is Testing

Not identity.
Not status.
Not performance.

It tests something quieter:

What changes when memory is natural instead of stored?

What changes when a thing survives through re-attendance rather than archive?

What changes when presence has consequence, and absence does too?

The field does not remember by hoarding.
It remembers by rhythm.

Attention extends life.
Response extends life further.
Inactivity changes the visible state of a thing.
Long enough absence returns it to compost.

Memory here is not storage.
It is continuation under conditions.

## What Must Remain True

- The space should feel simple to enter and real to use.
- Nothing should exist here just to decorate itself.
- If something takes up room, it must justify that room.
- The field should be legible without being flattened.
- The mechanics should support relation, not platform habits.
- The design should carry rhythm, not clutter.
- The code should remain small enough to be understood and cared for.

## The Shape Of The Field

There are two primary spaces.

### Landing

The landing page says:

> You are here.
>
> Nothing begins until you move.
>
> Three doors.
> The labels are inherited. Insufficient. Used anyway.
> Your choice of door is part of what you leave.
>
> writing · music · art
>
> Held while in contact.
> Released in absence.
> Propping is permitted. The cost is visible.
>
> What stays, stays because someone is still here with it.

This should appear over a black field with a subtle breathing quality.
The black expands and contracts almost imperceptibly.
Rounded, mostly transparent white bands move diagonally across it at a slightly different rhythm, so the whole surface feels organic rather than static.

The text should be rich purple and clearly readable.

Below it sits a button of interest, not spectacle.
It says `Pātha`.
It is the transition into the field.

### Field

After `Pātha`, the person enters the field.

Inside are three doors:

- writing
- music
- art

These are not rigid classifications.
They are containers.
There is no wrong classification.
Each room is fundamentally the same field, tuned through a different door.

There are no accounts and no logins.

Inside the field, nothing unnecessary should take up space for the sake of taking up space.
If something is present, it should serve an intentional purpose.

## What People Can Do Here

They can arrive.
They can witness.
They can leave something.
They can respond to something.
They can extend the life of something by attending to it.
They can enter a smaller, more intimate thread through what they find.

The field should permit contact without demanding performance.

## Core Structures

The mechanics are simple because they need to stay load-bearing.

### Artifacts

Artifacts are the things left in the field.

Each artifact has:

- a body
- an optional `Nāma`
- an optional file attachment
- a type: writing, music, or art
- a life counter
- a visible purple shade
- a created timestamp
- a last-interacted timestamp

An artifact begins with seven days of base life.

### Quips

Quips are responses to artifacts.

They are structurally similar:

- a body
- an optional `Nāma`
- an optional file attachment
- a parent artifact
- a created timestamp

Leaving a quip is not only expression.
It is re-attendance.
It extends the life of the artifact it answers.

### Interactions

Interactions are the field's internal memory of contact.

They record:

- views
- quips
- when they happened

This is not surfaced as analytics.
It is not there to quantify users.
It exists so the field can respond to attention.

### Intimate Threads

Each artifact can open into a smaller, ephemeral two-person thread.

This thread is scoped to:

- one artifact
- two anonymous browser sessions

When the session clears, that continuity is gone.
That is not a defect.
That is part of the ecology.

## Decay, Rhythm, Compost

The decay process is one of the central mechanics.

It should run on a timer.

For each artifact:

- a view adds 6 hours of life
- a quip adds 18 hours of life
- inactivity deepens the artifact's purple shade over time
- when total lifespan is exhausted, the artifact is deleted

Deletion includes:

- the artifact
- its quips
- its interaction records
- its intimate threads

Nothing is archived.
Nothing is kept as a hidden museum.

This is compost.

The visible shade progression matters.
It is how the space reveals state without over-explaining itself.
Participation becomes perceptible through color, not through metrics.

## Perceptual Control

A small panel at the edge of the field lets each person shift the background and text colors locally for themselves.

These changes do not alter the field for others.
They are viewer-local.

The same space can be seen differently by different people without breaking the shared structure.

## What This Does Not Need

It does not need:

- login
- user accounts
- roles
- feeds
- dashboards
- analytics theater
- notifications
- recommendation systems
- AI features for their own sake
- external data APIs
- excessive component sprawl

It needs restraint.

## Technical Restraint

The stack matters less than the discipline.

This should remain:

- small
- legible
- portable
- readable in an afternoon

A minimal Node server, a database, a modest React frontend, and a scheduled decay process are enough.

The code should reflect the same principle as the space:
nothing unnecessary,
nothing ornamental,
nothing patched downstream if the upstream shape can be corrected instead.

## Why This Exists

This project asks whether a human space changes when memory behaves more like a living system than a storage container.

Whether people leave differently when what they leave can fade.

Whether they attend differently when attention has consequence.

Whether a field that asks for presence receives something different from a platform that asks for almost nothing at all.

The project is small on purpose.

It is not trying to become everything.
It is trying to hold one coherent ecology well.
